Conclusion

The data charts provided has revealed a compelling correlation between the amount of time teenagers dedicated to the internet. Students who invest more hours on the internet tend to yield to have low or high GWA depends on how they use it. Girls spent so much time on the internet, yet they still get good grades. On the other hand, men spend more time on the internet but don't really get good grades. In our observation, this is because men use the internet to play online games or do unnecessary things. The digital age has provided an unprecedented platform for learning, socializing, and selfexpression. On one hand, the internet opens doors to a wealth of information, educational resources allowing the grade 9 students to broaden their horizons and connect with the world in ways that were unimaginable in previous generations. The value of internet, where it helps the students with the learning process as it helps to simplify the knowledge. In conclusion, the time Grade 9 students spend online is a double-edged sword, offering both opportunities and challenges. A concerted effort from educators and parents is necessary to strike a balance that combines the benefits of online resources while protecting student well-being. This approach aims to empower Grade 9 students to become responsible digital citizens, who can use the Internet for learning and personal growth while minimizing the potential risks associated with excessive online use.
